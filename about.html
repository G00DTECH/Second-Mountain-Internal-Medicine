About Maine Beacon

Filling a critical gap in Maine's homeless services landscape

Our Story

The Maine Winter Survival Initiative (MWSI) addresses a critical gap in Maine's homeless services landscape—the absence of any systematic program distributing survival equipment and MREs to the state's unsheltered population.

With Maine's 2025 Point-in-Time count showing 2,413 homeless individuals statewide, including 281 unsheltered persons facing life-threatening winter conditions, and with veteran homelessness increasing 10% this year, MWSI fills an urgent unmet need.

Founded by a veteran with housing authority expertise and a business strategist with financial analysis background, this nonprofit will launch in Portland within six months, expanding to Lewiston and Bangor within three years.

Mission Statement

To preserve lives and dignity by providing survival equipment and nourishment to homeless individuals in Maine's harshest conditions, while connecting them to pathways toward permanent housing.

Our Vision

A Maine where no person experiencing homelessness faces winter without the basic tools for survival, and where every unsheltered individual knows they are valued by their community.

Our Core Values

1

Dignity First

Every interaction treats recipients as neighbors deserving respect, not charity cases. Distribution practices emphasize choice, privacy, and autonomy over institutional efficiency.

2

Life-Saving Urgency

Maine winters kill. When temperatures threaten survival, bureaucratic barriers cannot delay response. MWSI maintains emergency deployment capability for severe weather events.

3

Pathway, Not Endpoint

Survival equipment sustains life; housing restores it. Every kit distribution includes information connecting recipients to Coordinated Entry, MaineHousing programs, and rapid rehousing services.

4

Veteran Commitment

Founded by a veteran who understands service and sacrifice, MWSI prioritizes reaching homeless veterans, who increased 10% in Maine's 2025 count despite overall homeless population decreases.

5

Community Integration

MWSI complements rather than competes with existing services, partnering with established organizations like Preble Street, Trinity Jubilee Center, and Bangor Area Homeless Shelter to extend their reach.

Our Approach

Targeted Distribution

We focus on unsheltered individuals who face the greatest exposure risk—those living in tents, vehicles, or exposed locations across Maine's harshest winter areas.

Partnership-Based

We work alongside existing homeless services organizations, extending their capacity rather than duplicating efforts or competing for resources.

No Barriers

No sobriety requirements, no identification needed, no curfews. We meet people where they are with the immediate support they need to survive.
