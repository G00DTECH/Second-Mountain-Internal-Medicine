What Makes Our Approach Different

1

Filling a Critical Gap

No dedicated program exists in Maine for distributing survival equipment or MREs to homeless populations. Shelters report daily requests for sleeping bags but lack budget to fulfill them.

2

Immediate Life-Saving Impact

Each survival kit provides protection to -30°F wind chills plus 3 days of nutrition. When distributed before severe weather, these kits directly prevent exposure deaths.

3

Partnership-Based Model

We extend the reach of established organizations like Preble Street and Trinity Jubilee Center rather than competing for resources or duplicating services.

4

Housing Pathway Integration

Every distribution connects individuals to Maine's Coordinated Entry system, MaineHousing programs, and rapid rehousing services—creating pathways beyond survival.
